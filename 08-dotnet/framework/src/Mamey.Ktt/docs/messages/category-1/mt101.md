### MT101 - Request for Transfer
#### Part 1: User Documentation
Purpose:
The MT101 message is used to request a financial institution to transfer funds from the account of the ordering customer to a beneficiary's account. This message type is commonly used for initiating payments, especially when a customer wants to instruct their bank to make a transfer on their behalf.

When to Use It:

To initiate a payment from a customerâ€™s account to another bank account.
To instruct a bank to transfer funds for business or personal payments.
Useful in scenarios where multiple transfers need to be processed in bulk.

Explanation of Fields:
F20 (Transaction Reference Number): A unique identifier assigned by the sender for tracking the transaction.
F21 (Related Reference): Refers to a previous transaction, if applicable.
F32B (Currency and Amount): Specifies the currency and amount to be transferred. For example, USD1000,00.
F50A (Ordering Customer): Details of the customer initiating the transfer, including their account number and name.
F59 (Beneficiary Customer): Information about the recipient of the funds, such as the account number, name, and address.
F70 (Remittance Information): Free-text information used to provide details about the payment (e.g., invoice number).
F71A (Details of Charges): Determines who bears the charges:
"BEN": Charges are borne by the beneficiary.
"OUR": Charges are borne by the sender.
"SHA": Charges are shared between sender and beneficiary.
F72 (Sender to Receiver Information): Optional field for additional instructions.
