## Technical Terms

### Blockchain Terms

**Block Lattice**: A blockchain architecture where each account has its own blockchain, enabling parallel processing and high throughput.

**DPoS (Delegated Proof-of-Stake)**: A consensus mechanism where token holders vote for delegates who validate transactions.

**Finality**: The point at which a transaction is considered irreversible and confirmed on the blockchain.

**TPS (Transactions Per Second)**: A measure of blockchain throughput, indicating how many transactions can be processed per second.

**Latency**: The time it takes for a transaction to be processed, measured from submission to confirmation.

---

## Business Terms

### Market Terms

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The total market demand for a product or service.

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The portion of TAM that can be served by a company's products or services.

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: The portion of SAM that a company can realistically capture.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**: The yearly value of recurring revenue from subscriptions or contracts.

## Platform-Specific Terms

### MameyNode Terms

**MameyNode**: Mamey Technologies' production-ready blockchain platform featuring Block Lattice architecture and DPoS consensus.

**Master Trust Accounts**: Special account types designed for compliance and regulatory requirements.

**Compliance-by-Design**: Built-in compliance features (AML/CFT, KYC, sanctions) that operate automatically on every transaction.

**35+ Modules**: Comprehensive blockchain functionality including 19 core modules and 16 specialized modules.

## Industry Terminology

### Financial Services Terms

**CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency)**: Digital currency issued by a central bank.

**RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement)**: A payment system that processes transactions in real-time on a gross basis.

**Cross-Border Payments**: Payments between parties in different countries.

**Trade Finance**: Financial instruments and products used to facilitate international trade.
