### SLIDE 3: THE GLOBAL FOOD SECURITY CHALLENGE
**Title:** Why Food Sovereignty Matters for Indigenous Nations
**Content:**
Indigenous communities worldwide face disproportionate food insecurity despite being traditional stewards of agricultural knowledge. Current statistics show that native populations experience food insecurity rates 2-3 times higher than national averages, with limited access to fresh, culturally appropriate foods. Climate change threatens traditional food systems, while industrial agriculture depletes soil health and biodiversity. The global food system is increasingly controlled by multinational corporations, leaving indigenous nations dependent on external supply chains vulnerable to disruption. Rising food prices disproportionately impact native communities, consuming larger percentages of household income. There is urgent need for indigenous-controlled agricultural systems that combine traditional knowledge with modern sustainable practices to achieve true food sovereignty and economic independence.

### SLIDE 6: REGENERATIVE AGRICULTURE PRACTICES
**Title:** Indigenous Knowledge Meets Modern Sustainability
**Content:**
The INKG Agriculture Program employs regenerative practices rooted in indigenous agricultural traditions that have sustained communities for millennia. **Polyculture Systems** (Three Sisters: corn, beans, squash) maximize land productivity while improving soil health. **Crop Rotation** prevents soil depletion and reduces pest pressure without chemicals. **Cover Cropping** builds organic matter and prevents erosion during off-seasons. **Integrated Pest Management** uses natural predators and companion planting instead of pesticides. **Water Conservation** through traditional irrigation methods and rainwater harvesting. **Seed Sovereignty** preserving heirloom varieties adapted to local conditions. These practices not only produce higher-quality food but also sequester carbon, restore biodiversity, and build long-term soil fertility, creating agricultural systems that improve rather than degrade the land over time.
