# NEXUS Academia — Technical Whitepaper

**Version**: 1.0.0 | **Date**: March 2026 | **Status**: Production

## Executive Summary

NEXUS Academia provides sovereign higher education infrastructure, enabling indigenous universities to operate independently of Western academic gatekeepers. It supports research collaboration, academic publishing, and indigenous knowledge systems within a culturally-appropriate framework.

## Problem Statement

Indigenous scholars are forced to publish through Western journals that charge access fees, research about indigenous peoples is conducted without community consent, and indigenous knowledge systems are excluded from academic recognition. Sovereign academic infrastructure ensures indigenous intellectual sovereignty.

## Solution Architecture

### University Platform
- Course management with indigenous pedagogical approaches
- Student enrollment and credential management
- Faculty collaboration and research tools
- Library system with indigenous collection priorities

### Research Infrastructure
- Collaborative research with community consent protocols
- Pre-print server for indigenous scholarship
- Peer review by indigenous academic community
- Data sovereignty for all research data

### Publishing
- Open-access journal platform (no paywalls)
- Indigenous language academic publishing
- Citation system recognizing oral traditions
- DOI-compatible with sovereign handle system

## Security Model

- Research data under researcher/community dual control
- Ethics review required for all indigenous knowledge research
- Plagiarism detection with indigenous attribution
- CARE principles (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics)

## Roadmap

- Q1 2026: University management system (DONE)
- Q2 2026: Research collaboration platform
- Q3 2026: Academic journal and publishing system
- Q4 2026: Cross-nation indigenous academic network
